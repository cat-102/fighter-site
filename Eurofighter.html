Eurofighter Typhoon 

 

 
 Primary function: Multirole fighter (air superiority / ground attack) 
 Contractor: Eurofighter GmbH (UK, Germany, Italy, Spain) 
 Power plant: Two Eurojet EJ200 afterburning turbofan engines 
 Thrust: 20,000 pounds (90 kN) each with afterburner 
 Length: 52.4 feet (16 meters) 
 Wingspan: 35.9 feet (10.95 meters) 
 Height: 17.3 feet (5.28 meters) 
 Maximum takeoff weight: 52,000 pounds (23,586 kilograms) 
 Speed: Mach 2.0+ (approx. 1,550 mph / 2,495 km/h at altitude) 
 Range: 1,800 miles (2,900 kilometers) with external fuel tanks 
 Combat radius: ~750 nautical miles (1,390 km) 
 Ceiling: 55,000 feet (16,764 meters) 
 Armament: 
   • One Mauser BK-27mm revolver cannon (150 rounds) 
   • Up to 16,500 lbs (7,500 kg) of ordnance across 13 hardpoints 
   • Air-to-air: AIM-9 Sidewinder, AIM-132 ASRAAM, AIM-120 AMRAAM, Meteor 
   • Air-to-ground: Paveway laser-guided bombs, Storm Shadow cruise missiles, 
     Brimstone, JDAM 
 Crew: 
   • Single-seat (Typhoon F2/FGR4) 
   • Twin-seat (Typhoon T1/T3 for training or strike missions) 
 Unit cost (approx): $100–125 million (varies by block and upgrades) 
 Initial operating capability: 2003 
 Inventory: Operated by UK (RAF), Germany (Luftwaffe), Italy, Spain, Austria, 
 Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait
